Brain Health

Understanding Your Brain

The brain is the most complex organ in the human body

Brain Structure

The brain consists of the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem, each controlling different functions from movement to vital processes.

  • Weighs about 1.4 kg
  • Contains 86 billion neurons
  • Uses 20% of body's oxygen

Brain Functions

Controls thoughts, memory, emotion, touch, motor skills, vision, breathing, temperature, and every process regulating our body.

  • Cognitive processing
  • Motor control
  • Sensory processing

Brain Protection

Protected by the skull, meninges (three layers of membranes), and cerebrospinal fluid that cushions against impacts.

  • Blood-brain barrier
  • Cerebrospinal fluid
  • Meningeal layers

Brain Health Tips

Maintain brain health through proper nutrition, regular exercise, adequate sleep, and mental stimulation activities.

  • Exercise regularly
  • Quality sleep (7-9 hours)
  • Mediterranean diet
Brain Disorders

Common Brain Diseases & Disorders

Understanding different neurological conditions

Neurodegenerative

Alzheimer's Disease

A progressive neurological disorder causing brain cell death, leading to memory loss and cognitive decline. Affects millions worldwide.

55M+ affected globally Source: WHO, 2023
Vascular

Stroke (Cerebrovascular Accident)

Occurs when blood supply to the brain is interrupted, causing brain cells to die. Leading cause of disability worldwide.

15M cases annually Source: WHO, 2023
Neurological

Epilepsy

A chronic neurological disorder characterized by recurrent seizures due to abnormal electrical activity in the brain.

50M people affected Source: WHO, 2023
Brain Tumors

Understanding Brain Tumors

What you need to know about brain tumors - from trusted medical sources

What is a Brain Tumor?

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells within the brain or the central spinal canal.

According to the American Brain Tumor Association, brain tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). The rigid structure of the skull means any abnormal growth can increase intracranial pressure, potentially leading to severe neurological damage.

Key Fact: Early detection and accurate classification of brain tumors are critical for selecting appropriate treatment strategies and improving patient outcomes.

Types of Brain Tumors We Detect

Glioma

Origin: Glial Cells

Tumors originating in the glial cells that surround and support neurons. Includes astrocytomas, oligodendrogliomas, and glioblastomas.

Primary Brain Tumor Can be aggressive

Source: National Cancer Institute (NCI)

Meningioma

Origin: Meninges

Tumors forming on the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. Most meningiomas are benign and slow-growing.

Mostly Benign Common in women

Source: American Association of Neurological Surgeons

Pituitary Tumor

Origin: Pituitary Gland

Abnormal growths in the pituitary gland at the base of the brain. Can affect hormone production and cause various symptoms.

Hormone Effects Usually benign

Source: National Institutes of Health (NIH)

No Tumor

Normal Brain Scan

MRI scans showing no evidence of brain tumors. Regular check-ups are still recommended for preventive health.

Healthy Preventive care

Regular screening recommended
